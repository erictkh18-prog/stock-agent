# Extracted Claims
- Market movement is multi-factor: macro data, policy expectations, earnings trajectory, liquidity, and positioning all interact.
- Interest-rate expectations and bond-yield direction often drive valuation changes, especially in long-duration growth sectors.
- Inflation surprises can trigger cross-asset repricing and sector rotation between defensives and cyclicals.
- Earnings revisions and guidance changes act as short-horizon catalysts that can override macro narratives.
- Geopolitical and commodity shocks can alter volatility regimes and risk premium assumptions.
- Positioning and sentiment extremes increase squeeze risk; price reaction around catalysts matters more than headline tone.

# Actionable Rules Derived
- Build a market map before execution: classify environment as inflation-led, growth-led, policy-led, or shock-driven.
- Reduce gross exposure ahead of major macro releases unless a catalyst strategy with predefined risk is active.
- Use yield-curve and real-yield trend as regime filters for sector selection and holding horizon.
- Confirm narrative with price structure: enter only when trend, breadth, and volume align with thesis.
- Treat earnings season as a separate volatility regime; tighten stops and avoid oversized single-name risk.
- Monitor sentiment/positioning indicators to avoid chasing crowded momentum into reversal risk.

# Trading Application Notes
